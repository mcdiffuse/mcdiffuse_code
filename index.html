Anonymous Submission: MC-DiffSE

This repository contains the anonymous submission of our work titled "Clarity or Confusion: The Double-Edged Sword of Diffusion-based Speech Enhancement".

Abstract

Diffusion-based generative models (DGMs) have recently garnered lots of attention also in the speech processing field following the promising results achieved in computer vision. Recent studies suggest that DGMs have better generalization and produces less intrusive artifacts than discriminative methods. However, to our best knowledge, no systematic experimental review exists on this subject with a fairer comparison with state-of-the-art (SotA) discriminative models. In this work we test the generalization capability of DGM for SE, comparing three different models on 4 datasets with mismatch characteristics. We evaluate the performance using signal level, perceptual and semantical characteristics of the enhanced speech 8 metrics.Our results show that DGMs methods do not have yet a clear advantage over SotA discriminative ones for SE and are also more prone to hallucinating the content of the speech especially in highly mismatched conditions such as real-world spontaneous speech.
